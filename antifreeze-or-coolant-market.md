<p><p>Antifreeze or coolant markets typically consist of three main types: Ethylene Glycol (EG), Propylene Glycol (PG), and Glycerin. EG is the most commonly used antifreeze due to its low cost and ability to provide effective freeze protection. PG is often chosen for its non-toxic properties, making it safer for environments with potential contact with humans or animals. Glycerin is a less common option but is sometimes preferred for its biodegradability and compatibility with certain materials. Each type of antifreeze has its own unique advantages and applications in the market.</p></p>
