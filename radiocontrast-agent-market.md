<p><p>Radiocontrast agents are substances used in medical imaging to enhance the visibility of internal structures. There are four main types of radiocontrast agents: Barium-based, which are used primarily for gastrointestinal imaging; Iodinated, commonly used for X-ray and CT scans; Gadolinium-based, used in MRI scans to improve contrast; and Microbubble agents, which contain gas bubbles and are used in ultrasound imaging. Each type of agent has specific properties that make them suitable for different imaging modalities and anatomical areas.</p></p>
