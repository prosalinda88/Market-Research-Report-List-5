<p><p>Diaphragm Accumulators are commonly categorized into two types: Spring Accumulator and Gas Accumulator. Spring Accumulators use a spring force to compress the diaphragm and store energy, while Gas Accumulators use gas (usually nitrogen) to store pressure energy. Spring Accumulators are typically used in applications where high pressure fluid storage is required, while Gas Accumulators are commonly used in hydraulic systems to improve system response and stability. Both types of accumulators play a crucial role in various industries such as automotive, aerospace, and industrial machinery.</p></p>
