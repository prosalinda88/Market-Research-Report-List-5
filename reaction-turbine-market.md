<p><p>Reaction turbines are a type of turbine used in hydroelectric power plants to convert the energy of flowing water into mechanical energy. There are two main types of reaction turbines based on the direction of flow through the turbine blades: axial flow and radial flow turbines. </p><p>Axial flow turbines have blades that move parallel to the direction of flow, while radial flow turbines have blades that move perpendicular to the direction of flow. Both types of turbines are commonly used in power generation, but each has its own advantages and limitations in terms of efficiency and performance.</p></p>
