<p><p>Laboratory filtration technology encompasses various types of filtration methods including Microfiltration Technology, Reverse Osmosis Technology, Ultrafiltration Technology, Vacuum Filtration Technology, and Nanofiltration Technology. Microfiltration uses a physical barrier to separate particles, Reverse Osmosis utilizes a semipermeable membrane to remove ions and molecules, Ultrafiltration removes larger molecules, Vacuum Filtration applies vacuum pressure to separate solids from liquids, and Nanofiltration is used for fine particle separation. Each technology serves a specific purpose in laboratory filtration processes.</p></p>
