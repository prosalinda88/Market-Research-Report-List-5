<p><p>Optical flame detectors are classified into two main types: Infrared (IR) detectors and Ultraviolet (UV) detectors. IR detectors are sensitive to infrared radiation emitted by flames, making them suitable for detecting flames where smoke or other particles may obscure visibility. UV detectors, on the other hand, are sensitive to ultraviolet radiation produced by flames, making them effective for detecting flames in environments with high background radiation. Both types of detectors play a crucial role in ensuring timely and accurate detection of fires for improved safety and protection.</p></p>
