<p><p>Pregelatinized wheat starches are divided into two main market types based on their viscosity levels. Low viscosity wheat starches are ideal for use in applications where a thinner consistency is desired, such as soups and sauces. On the other hand, high viscosity wheat starches are better suited for products that require a thicker texture, like puddings and pie fillings. Both types offer the convenience of instant thickening without the need for cooking, making them popular ingredients in the food industry.</p></p>
