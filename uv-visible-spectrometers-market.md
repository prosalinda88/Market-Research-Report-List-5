<p><p>Single-beam UV-Vis spectrophotometers are instruments that measure the amount of light absorbed by a sample at a specific wavelength, comparing it to a reference beam. Double-beam UV-Vis spectrophotometers work on a similar principle, but with the added advantage of comparing the sample beam and reference beam simultaneously. Both types of spectrometers are widely used in various industries such as pharmaceuticals, environmental monitoring, and food and beverage for analyzing the concentration of substances in samples based on their absorption of ultraviolet and visible light.</p></p>
