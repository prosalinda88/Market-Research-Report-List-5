Hyperfiltration involves removing contaminants at the molecular level, while microfiltration is used for larger particles. Nanofiltration is a more specialized filtration process that separates particles on a nanometer scale. Each type of membrane market serves a specific purpose in industries such as water treatment, pharmaceuticals, and food & beverage production. Together, they offer a wide range of options for achieving precise filtration and separation goals.</p></p>
