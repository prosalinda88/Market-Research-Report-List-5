<p><p>Artillery fuzes, used to detonate artillery shells at the desired time and distance, come in two main types: mechanical and electronic. Mechanical fuzes rely on physical mechanisms like springs and timers to function, while electronic fuzes use electronic components to precisely control detonation. Both types offer advantages and disadvantages - mechanical fuzes are generally simpler and more reliable, while electronic fuzes allow for greater precision and flexibility. The choice between the two types depends on factors such as cost, sophistication of the weapon system, and specific mission requirements.</p></p>
