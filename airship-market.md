<p><p>Airships are classified into three main market types based on their structure: rigid, semi-rigid, and non-rigid. Rigid airships have a solid framework that maintains their shape, providing stability and allowing for larger payloads. Semi-rigid airships have a partial framework, offering a balance between stability and flexibility. Non-rigid airships, also known as blimps, have no internal structure and rely on the pressure of the lifting gas to maintain their shape. Each market type offers unique advantages and applications in industries such as transportation, advertising, and surveillance.</p></p>
