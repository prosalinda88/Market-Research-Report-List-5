<p><p>Craft beer market types can be categorized into two main segments: ales and lagers. Ales are typically brewed with top-fermenting yeast at warmer temperatures, resulting in a diverse range of flavors from citrusy and fruity to malty and nutty. Lagers, on the other hand, are brewed with bottom-fermenting yeast at cooler temperatures, producing crisp and clean profiles with a lighter body. Both ales and lagers cater to different consumer preferences, offering a wide variety of options in the craft beer market.</p></p>
