<p><p>Battery-grade graphite is primarily classified into two types: Natural graphite and artificial graphite. Natural graphite is sourced from graphite deposits in the earth's crust and processed for use in batteries. It is known for its high purity levels, which make it ideal for lithium-ion batteries. On the other hand, artificial graphite is produced through a complex manufacturing process using petroleum coke as a precursor material. It offers better performance characteristics compared to natural graphite, making it a preferred choice for high-end applications in the battery industry.</p></p>
